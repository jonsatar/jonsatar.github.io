A Guiding Philosophy - The Scientific Method

The scientific method makes no initial assumptions, only careful observations regarding natural phenomena.


A phenomenon is a natural or artificial event, in the broadest of senses. For the purposes of this course, we will consider an event to be the biological entity, that has a momentary existence, much like any other event we observe.


If there is a pattern in these observations, we may make generalizations in concise statements called natural laws, which are derived through this inductive process.


That is to say, inductive logical processes, in a loose sense, involve making a generalization or conclusion from the set of data.


The natural laws are often very simple verbal, or better yet, mathematical statements. They are well- established theories that have both explanatory and predictive powers as well. This is the ultimate goal of the scientific method.


Experimentation is a crucial component of verifying inductive reasoning.


We design our experiments as a means to test our intial notion, that is the hypothesis. Thereby, allowing for its acceptance or rejection without bias.


The scientific method, used in our study of biology, somewhat contrasts the deductive logical processes often encountered in more precise disciplines such as mathematics.


Deductive logical processes have certain conclusions that logically follow from established and known premises.


Let us begin by using the scientific method as a heuristic to begin understanding some of the early developments in biology, that lay the groundwork for the intensive discipline it is today.


In the investigation below, let us investigate, does the presence of capsaicin in chilies deter some predators but not others?

Biological Timeline

In the year 1500 ...
Leonardo Da Vinci creates anatomical drawings from his dissections of the human body.
In the year 1628...
William Harvey describes the anatomical function of the heart and the circulation of blood in animals.
In the year 1674 ...
Anton van Loeuwenhoek fashions more elaborate lenses to increase the microscope's magnification to visualize, red blood cells, sperm, and bacteria. This led to the development of the cell theory.
In the year 1809 ...
Jean Baptiste Lamarck is the first to propose the theory of evolution based upon the inheritance of acquired characteristics.
In the year 1864 ...
Louis Pasteur refutes the theory of spontaneous generation in his landmark experiment.
In the year 1859...
Charles Darwin proposes the natural selection as the mechanism for the theory of Evolution
In the year 1866... Gregor Mendel revolutionsizes genetics with his experiments on heritability in pea plants
In the year 1898 ...
Martinus Beljerink discovers the virus, a somewhat living entity much smaller than the known bacterium at the time.
In the year 1928...
Alexander Fleming discovers penicillin.
In the year 1953 ...
Watson and Crick deduce the helical structure of DNA.
